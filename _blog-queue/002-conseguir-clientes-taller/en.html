How to get more customers for your repair shop

You don't need a big ad budget. Most repair shop customers arrive through local search and recommendation. Here is what really works.

📅 June 7, 2026⏱ 7 min read

In this article

  1. Word of mouth isn't enough
  2. Your Google listing is the storefront
  3. Reviews sell for you
  4. WhatsApp and social without overdoing it
  5. Frequently asked questions

When someone breaks their phone, the first thing they do is search "phone repair near me". If your shop doesn't show up well there, you don't exist for that customer. The good news: ranking locally is within reach of any shop.

1. Word of mouth isn't enough

Word of mouth is still gold, but today it happens online: instead of asking a neighbour, people read reviews and check the map. Your job is to be visible when they search and to look trustworthy.

2. Your Google listing is the storefront

The Google Business Profile (the box on the map) is your most powerful tool and it's free. To make the most of it:

  • Fill everything: hours, phone, address, shop photos and services.
  • Right category: "mobile phone repair shop".
  • Real photos: of the shop and jobs; they build more trust than a logo.
  • Answer messages: Google penalises listings that don't reply.

Tip: a well-kept Google listing can bring more customers than any paid ad, at no cost.

3. Reviews sell for you

Reviews are the social proof that convinces the customer. Don't leave them to chance:

  • Ask for the review at handover, when the customer is happy
  • Make it easy: a QR or a direct link
  • Reply to all of them, including negatives, politely
  • Note who reviews to build loyalty

4. WhatsApp and social without overdoing it

WhatsApp is the favourite channel to notify that the phone is ready and to answer quick questions. Use it professionally with a business number. On social, you don't need to post daily: show real repairs, before and after, and occasional promotions.

Frequently asked questions

How much should I spend on ads?
Before paying for ads, squeeze the free stuff: a complete Google listing, reviews and WhatsApp. For most small shops, that already brings enough customers. Paid ads make sense once the above is optimised.
How do I get more reviews?
Ask at handover, when the customer is satisfied, and make it easy with a QR or direct link. Asking naturally works much better than waiting for them to arrive on their own.
Do I need social media?
It helps, but it isn't essential. A well-kept Google listing and good reviews weigh more. If you use social, prioritise quality over quantity: real repairs and occasional promotions.
