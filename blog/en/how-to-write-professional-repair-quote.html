How to Write a Professional Repair Quote

A well-written quote is more than a price on paper: it is the first impression your shop makes, your legal protection against disputes, and the key to getting more customers to say yes.

Many shops treat the quote as a formality: they write a price on paper, the customer nods and that is it. But a poorly written quote is the root of the most common problems in a repair shop: customers arguing about the price when they collect, disputes about what was agreed, or repairs that end up costing more with no prior warning.

A professional quote, on the other hand, builds trust, reduces conflict and — when well presented — converts more hesitant customers into ones who say yes.

1. Why the quote matters more than you think

  • Legal protection: in most countries, consumer protection law requires the shop to inform the customer of the estimated cost before starting the repair. A signed quote is your shield against any future claim.
  • First impression: a customer who receives a clear, detailed quote with your shop logo perceives far more professionalism than one who gets a verbal price or a handwritten note.
  • Sales tool: a well-presented quote with a clear breakdown can tip the scales when the customer is deciding between repairing or buying new.

2. What a professional quote must include

  • Shop name, address and tax registration number
  • Quote number (for tracking and reference)
  • Issue date and expiry date
  • Customer details (name, phone, email)
  • Device description (brand, model, IMEI or serial number)
  • Detailed description of the reported fault
  • Work to be carried out and parts to be replaced (itemised)
  • Labour cost, parts cost and tax shown separately
  • Total price including tax
  • Terms: repair warranty and uncollected device policy
  • Customer signature (quote acceptance)

Important: the IMEI or serial number is essential. In any future dispute, it is the only objective proof that the device you handed back is the same one you received.

4. How to calculate and present the price

Always itemise, even if the customer does not ask

Separating the part cost from the labour cost helps the customer understand where the price comes from and makes acceptance easier.

  • The customer understands the high price is the cost of the original part, not your margin.
  • If they opt for an aftermarket part, you can adjust the quote easily.
  • In case of a dispute, you have documented exactly what was charged for each item.

Part quality options

Offering two or three quality tiers turns the quote into a sales tool:

TierPartPriceWarranty
EconomyCompatible aftermarketLower3 months
StandardCertified refurbishedMid6 months
PremiumOriginal OEMHigher12 months

Offering options does not mean the customer will always choose the cheapest. Many choose the middle or even the premium tier — this is the price anchoring effect.

Tip: when the part cost is high, always mention what a new device would cost. That puts the price in context and makes acceptance much easier.

5. How to get more quote acceptances

Quote acceptance rate has a direct impact on revenue. These are the factors that matter most:

  • Speed of diagnosis: the sooner you deliver the quote, the more likely the customer is to accept it.
  • Clear presentation: a quote with your logo and well structured builds far more confidence than a handwritten one.
  • Visible warranty: "3-month warranty included" is a selling point, not a footnote.
  • Explain the process: a customer who understands the work perceives more value.
  • Enable remote acceptance: if the customer can accept by WhatsApp or email, you remove the friction of returning just to sign.

6. What to do when unexpected issues arise

Sometimes, when opening the device, additional damage appears that was not visible in the initial diagnosis. In that case, never continue without contacting the customer first.

The correct process is:

  1. Stop work immediately when you detect the unexpected issue.
  2. Contact the customer (a call is preferable to leave a verbal record).
  3. Explain what you found and what it means in terms of cost and time.
  4. Issue a revised quote and get written approval (a WhatsApp message counts as evidence).
  5. Only then continue with the repair.

Golden rule: you cannot exceed the accepted quote without the customer's explicit authorisation. If you do, you cannot legally require them to pay the difference.

Frequently asked questions

Is a written quote required before starting a repair?
In most countries, consumer protection law requires the shop to inform the customer of the estimated cost before starting. A written and signed quote is always the best practice.
What happens if the repair costs more than quoted?
You must contact the customer before continuing and get authorisation for the new amount. You can never exceed the accepted quote without prior approval.
How long is a repair quote valid?
There is no universal legal requirement, but the common practice is 7 to 15 days. After that, part prices may have changed.
Can the shop charge a diagnosis fee?
Yes, as long as the customer is informed beforehand. A diagnosis fee that is deducted from the total if the repair is accepted is common practice.
What is the difference between a quote and a proforma invoice?
A quote is a price offer with no tax effect. A proforma invoice has invoice format and is used for corporate repairs or when the customer needs to justify the cost in advance.
